# Filippi Somatic Method — Framework Reference

Source: "The Chronobiological Architecture of the Somatic Method" — analysis of Dr. Mark Filippi's lunar-neurotransmitter theory. Full document in project files.

## Core Premise

The lunar cycle (29.5-day synodic month) acts as an environmental metronome, entraining the human nervous system through the rhythmic dominance of four neurotransmitters. Biological efficiency is a fluctuating variable — not a fixed trait — dependent on coherence with these external cycles.

The model is **fractal**: the same four-phase sequence repeats at every scale (breath, hour, day, month, season, life).

---

## The Four Quadrants

| Q | Neurotransmitter | Moon | Season | Orientation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Q1 | Acetylcholine | New moon | Spring | Receptivity, seeding, bonding, felt sense |
| Q2 | Serotonin | First quarter | Summer | Concentration, logic, cultivation, stamina |
| Q3 | Dopamine | Full moon | Autumn | Arousal, performance, expression, reward |
| Q4 | Norepinephrine | Last quarter | Winter | Analysis, vigilance, reflection, protection |

### Q1 — Acetylcholine
New Moon → First Quarter / Spring / Youth
- Synaptic plasticity, memory formation, parasympathetic nervous system
- Heightened emotional felt sense; emotions are more intense
- Natural inclination toward group bonding and social connection
- Not a time for high-output — a time for **planting**
- Corresponds to menstruation / return to biological roots

### Q2 — Serotonin
First Quarter → Full Moon / Summer / Young Adult
- Mood stability, impulse control, executive function
- Peak lucidity and personal cultivation
- Sustained mental concentration and creative productivity
- Orderly, disciplined energy — optimal for **skill acquisition**
- Corresponds to follicular phase / increasing energy reserves

### Q3 — Dopamine
Full Moon → Last Quarter / Autumn / Midlife
- Reward, motivation, arousal, pleasure-seeking
- Drive for external engagement at its peak
- High energy but risk of distraction and burnout
- Optimal for performance, public expression, social events
- Corresponds to ovulation / peak luminosity

### Q4 — Norepinephrine
Last Quarter → New Moon / Winter / Elder
- Vigilance, analysis, fight-or-flight modulation
- Defensive and analytical orientation
- Deep reflection, auditing, completing wrap-up phases
- Internal work, conservation of resources
- Corresponds to luteal phase / protective posture

## Fractal Scaling

The four-phase sequence is observable at every temporal scale:

| Scale | Q1 | Q2 | Q3 | Q4 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Lunar (29.5d) | New moon | First quarter | Full moon | Last quarter |
| Seasonal | Spring | Summer | Autumn | Winter |
| Daily (24h) | Morning | Midday | Afternoon | Evening/Night |
| Breath | Inhale | Inhale peak | Exhale | Exhale rest |

An individual may be in a dopamine week (lunar) but a serotonin hour (daily). Their state is a non-linear product of these overlapping waves.

## Alignment Note for Mood Ring

- Filippi: Season cycle begins at **Spring** (Q1/acetylcholine)
